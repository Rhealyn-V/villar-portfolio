## 9. Suggestions to Strengthen Your Personal Brand

1. **Fill every placeholder.** Recruiters trust specifics — real company names, dates,
   your phone number, and school. Vague resumes get skipped.
2. **Add numbers.** Turn duties into results: "Automated payroll for X employees,"
   "Reduced billing errors by X%," "Cut report prep time from X hours to Y minutes."
3. **Ship your GitHub.** Push these 4 projects to GitHub with clear READMEs and screenshots,
   then link them in `src/data/portfolio.js` (repoUrl) so the "Code" buttons go live.
4. **Deploy live demos.** Host at least one project (Vercel/Netlify) and add the `liveUrl`.
   A clickable demo beats a description every time.
5. **Get 2–3 real certifications** and list them (e.g., freeCodeCamp Responsive Web Design,
   Google IT Support, or a bookkeeping cert). Update the Certificates section as you go.
6. **Collect testimonials.** Ask a past manager or client for 1–2 sentences; replace the
   placeholders in the testimonials array.
7. **Consistent handle.** Use the same name/handle across GitHub, LinkedIn, and email so
   people can find you. Add a professional photo everywhere (you already have a great one).
8. **Write, a little.** A short post on how you built the payroll automation shows expertise
   and feeds your SEO. Even one good LinkedIn post helps.
9. **Custom domain.** Consider `rhealynvillar.dev` or `.com` — it looks far more professional
   than a default subdomain and is memorable on a resume.
10. **Keep the niche clear.** Your edge is "developer who understands business operations and
    bookkeeping." Lead with that — it sets you apart from generic junior developers.
